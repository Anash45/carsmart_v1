What do you know about Audi?

When VW bought Auto Union from Daimler-Benz in the 1960s, the current Audi era officially began. Following the 1965 release of the Audi F103 series, which revitalized the Audi brand, Volkswagen combined Auto Union with NSU Motorenwerke in 1969 to become the firm that exists today.

The Latin equivalent of the founder August Horch's surname served as the basis for the firm name. In Latin, horch (which means "listen") becomes audi. The four rings in the Audi emblem stand for the four automakers that joined forces to form Auto Union, the firm that preceded Audi.

Not just any German automaker, but one of the top brands in the world of high-end vehicles is Audi. The vast range of fashionable Audi vehicles, from sporty sports cars to family-friendly saloons, contributes to the brand's appeal. But what really sets Audi apart are the clever minds that go into every piece of bodywork. The company has created advances in fields like ultralight design that are leading the market.

Audi demonstrates under the hood that they live up to their catchphrase, "Vorsprung Durch Technik" (Progress Through Technology). These days, the greatest electric cars available are Audi models, which are examples of this. Thanks to its cutting-edge technology, Audi can now really compete with other luxury companies like Mercedes and BMW.
